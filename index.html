"A tradition unlike any other..."

And so, as the Georgia sun sets on Augusta National, a champion emerges from the azaleas. JJ Guajardo has done it. With a masterful portfolio anchored by Rory McIlroy's historic back-to-back green jacket performance and the ever-steady Scottie Scheffler finishing solo second, JJ's picks proved to be nothing short of brilliant. Justin Rose's quietly magnificent T3 finish sealed the deal; a $8.56 million combined haul that will be very difficult to top.

Pat and Benny gave valiant efforts, carried by the McIlroy-Scheffler tandem, but in the end, it was JJ's faith in Rose and the shrewd late swap of Bryson DeChambeau for Jordan Spieth that made all the difference.

And then there's Gabe. Finishing dead last at $1.13 million with a roster that featured three missed cuts and Jon Rahm sleepwalking to a T38. Gabe, buddy, we've seen better lineups at a mini-golf tournament. We look forward to your redemption arc at the U.S. Open.

— The 18th Tower, Augusta National
